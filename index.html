FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

How does online coaching actually work?
Everything happens through ongoing message support, weekly check-ins, and personalized plans delivered directly to the athlete. You don't need to be in the same city. Athletes across North America and Europe have gone through Prodigy entirely remotely.
Will this work for my athlete?
If your athlete is committed, coachable, and ready to work, yes. Prodigy is built for players who want more than talent. If they are not ready to be honest, accountable, and do the work, this is not the right fit.
What if my athlete struggles with inconsistency, fear of mistakes, or confidence dips?
That is exactly what Prodigy is built for. Inconsistency and fear of mistakes are not talent problems. They are structure and mental rep problems. That is what we fix.
What ages is this for?
Prodigy is built for athletes aged 12 and up who are playing organized competitive hockey. If your athlete is under 12, the focus should be on enjoying the game and building confidence naturally.
What is Phase 1?
Phase 1 is a fully personalized 3-month foundation. It covers training, nutrition, recovery, mindset, leadership, on-ice skill analysis, and ongoing support. It is the starting point for every athlete who enters Prodigy.
How much support does my athlete get?
Ongoing text and voice message support, weekly check-ins, and active engagement throughout the program. This is high-touch mentorship, not a course you log into once a week.
How much does it cost?
Investment details are discussed on the call. Prodigy is a high-intent program and the investment reflects that. If you are committed to your athlete's development, book a call and we will find out if it is a fit.
What if my athlete is injured?
We continue working. Injury is part of sport. The mindset, leadership, nutrition, and structure work does not stop because of an injury. In many cases this is the most important time to have support.
